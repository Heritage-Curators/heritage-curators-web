\# Art Appreciation: Digital Museum Website Project



This document outlines the requirements, guidelines, and evaluation criteria for the \*\*ART 002 P3 Performance Task Exam: Digital Museum Website Project\*\*.



\---



\## Project Overview



The goal of this project is to create a virtual art exhibit that showcases artworks from a museum you have visited.  

The exhibit aims to promote Philippine culture while demonstrating skills in curation, art analysis, and digital presentation.



\---



\## Objectives



\- \*\*Research and Exploration:\*\* Investigate Philippine art and cultural heritage through documented museum visits.  

\- \*\*Curatorial Development:\*\* Select and organize artworks into a coherent thematic virtual exhibit.  

\- \*\*Critical Analysis:\*\* Apply principles of art appreciation to interpret the subject, content, and context of artworks.  

\- \*\*Digital Communication:\*\* Create an interactive and engaging website to present the findings.  



\---



\## Project Requirements \& Steps



\### 1. Research and Documentation

\- \*\*Document the Experience:\*\* Take clear photographs of artworks, architectural details, and exhibition spaces.  

\- \*\*Record Basic Info:\*\* Collect titles, artists, dates, locations, mediums, and cultural significance on-site.  

\- \*\*Conduct Research:\*\* Use credible sources to deepen understanding of the selected pieces.  



\### 2. Exhibit Curation

\- \*\*Select Artworks:\*\* Highlight at least 5 significant artworks.  

\- \*\*Thematic Focus:\*\* Choose artworks that represent a common theme or narrative (e.g., identity, tradition, colonial influence).  

\- \*\*Curatorial Statement:\*\* Write a short statement explaining the theme, why the artworks were selected, and the intended viewer experience.  



\### 3. Art Analysis \& Interpretation



For every selected piece, provide:



\- \*\*Analysis:\*\* Define the \*\*Subject\*\* (what is depicted), \*\*Content\*\* (message/emotion), and \*\*Context\*\* (historical/cultural background).  

\- \*\*Art Criticism:\*\* Use appropriate vocabulary to describe design elements and design principles based on visual evidence.  

\- \*\*Interpretive Labels:\*\* Create concise captions to guide the viewer.  



\### 4. Website Development

\- \*\*Platform Options:\*\* Wix, Google Sites, Canva, Carrd, WordPress, or Visual Studio Code.  

\- \*\*Structure:\*\* Include an introduction, gallery pages, artwork descriptions, and reflections.  

\- \*\*Interactive Features:\*\* Image galleries, clickable menus, embedded videos, timelines, or hyperlinks.  

\- \*\*Quality Control:\*\* Ensure images are properly sized, text is readable, and layout is consistent.

\## Rubrics for Evaluation



| Criteria         | 10 - Outstanding | 8 - Good | 5 - Fair | 2 - Poor |

|-----------------|------------------|----------|----------|----------|

| \*\*Research\*\*     | Complete list of artworks with correct facts and high-quality photos. | Most facts and photos are correct and look good. | Some facts missing or photos are blurry/unclear. | Very few facts or photos; many mistakes. |

| \*\*Curation\*\*     | Tells a clear "long-lasting" story; artworks organized perfectly. | Website is organized and easy to follow. | Website feels a bit messy or unorganized. | No organization; random photos with no theme. |

| \*\*Analysis\*\*     | Deeply explained meaning and history behind every piece of art. | Explained basic meaning and history of the art. | Only described what the art looks like, not what it means. | No explanation or copied from the internet. |

| \*\*Website Skills\*\* | Site is interactive, professional, and very easy to use. | Site works well and looks clean. | Site is okay but hard to navigate or "glitchy." | Site is broken, messy, or very hard to read. |
