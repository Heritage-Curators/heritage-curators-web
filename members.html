Group Reflection

What the team learned through the project

These reflections summarize how the museum-based research process shaped our understanding of Philippine art, teamwork, and interpretation.

On Viewing the Artworks

As a group, we learned that artworks reveal more when we slow down and study both what is shown and what is implied. Looking carefully at composition, mood, symbols, and historical setting helped us move from simple description to meaningful interpretation.

On Philippine Culture and History

The selected paintings deepened our appreciation of Philippine identity by showing rural life, colonial encounters, social struggle, beauty, and resilience. The project reminded us that art is not only decorative; it also preserves memory and reflects national experience.

On Collaborative Curation

Creating the website taught us to divide tasks responsibly while keeping one shared curatorial direction. Research, interpretation, design, and web development had to work together so the final exhibition would be clear, consistent, and engaging for viewers.

Visit Notes

How observation supported the website content

The project combined direct viewing, factual recording, and later analysis so each entry could balance visual description with historical meaning.

Observation

We paid attention to visual details such as light, scale, gesture, and atmosphere to record what each artwork communicated before writing our interpretations.

Documentation

We organized titles, artists, dates, mediums, and present locations so the website would not rely only on opinion, but also on clear factual information.

Interpretation

After gathering observations, we connected each work to Philippine history, identity, and social meaning to produce stronger analysis and more thoughtful gallery labels.
